Trading Strategies

There are a number of ways which you can use option data for a trading strategy. There are are discussed on trading strategies page.

  • First there is max pain. Stock options can be predictive since they hinge on the stock price being above or below the strike price on expiration. This is the heart of max pain.
  • Open Interest Walls are a similar strategy, but offers a range of potential closes on expiration.
  • Option Implied Volatility provides one standard deviation values for the expected change in a stocks price, it is 68% likely to remain in that range until expiration. Two standard deviations range is 95% likely.
  • The option greek delta provides a rough estimate of how likely a option is to be in or out of the money on expiration. For example, an option with a 0.5 delta is 50% likely to be in the money. A delta of 0.3 is 30% likely to be in the money.

Just show me the data

There are many option data charts available. Many of the charts show the entire range of strikes for a stock and a maturity so you can see everything at a glance.

  • The options charts display the open interest and volume for all strikes. You can easily see what strikes have a high amount of open interest. Plus, there is a chart of the cash value max pain which shows the predicted close price on expiration.
  • The implied volatility charts display the IV for all the strikes. And it calculates one and two standard deviation ranges demonstrating the likelihood of the stock closing within the range. Plus, there is a call option chart showing the breakdown of intrinsic value and time value at each strike.
  • The greeks charts display the delta, gamma, theta, vega and rho values for all the strike. And there is a gamma differential chart too.
  • The history charts display the daily changes in price, open interest and volume for a stock, maturity and strike price.

Tell me more about max pain

In a nutshell, max pain theory says that the option sellers (called writers) have stock on hand to fulfill the options if they are exercised. These stock positions are maintained to minimize risk to the sellers, and this maintenance affects the stock price. So, options can be the tail wagging the dog.
